End-user manual: requesting a TARF or NTARF

This guide is for faculty and staff who submit TARF (Travel Activity Request Form) or NTARF (Non-Travel Activity Request Form) through the WPU Faculty Portal.

1. Where to go

  1. Sign in to the Faculty Portal with your account.
  2. Open the sidebar and click TARF Request (also titled “Activity requests — TARF & NTARF”).
  3. At the top of the page, use the tabs: TARF (Travel) or NTARF (Non-travel).
  4. Use My requests to open your recent submissions (up to 15), including pending and rejected.

2. TARF vs NTARF — which form?

Form Use it when
TARF You or listed personnel need travel (off-site: conference, training, official business elsewhere, etc.). The system can apply attendance rules suited to travel.
NTARF The activity is non-travel — on-campus, or a declared venue/meeting setup. You must still use the timekeeper (time in, lunch out, lunch in, time out) on each activity day. NTARF approval does not replace DTR/timekeeper logs.
Important for NTARF File one NTARF per discrete activity (single session, venue, or time block). Mixed venues usually need separate forms.

3. After you submit — approval workflow

Routing is parallel where noted: several offices can act without a fixed sequence until all required endorsements exist.

  • Immediate supervisor (or college/office account you enter) must endorse.
  • Applicable endorser (you choose from the list) must endorse.
  • If your answers require fund certification (see forms below): Budget or Accounting must also endorse.
  • When all required endorsements are complete, the request goes to the President for final approval.
  • Statuses you may see include “Awaiting endorsements,” “With President (final),” “Approved,” or “Rejected.”

4. Tracking, print, and PDF

  • My requests lists recent submissions; open one to preview the printable layout.
  • For your own pending request, progress may show parallel lines such as Supervisor, Applicable endorser, and Budget/Accounting when applicable.
  • After status is fully approved (endorsed), use Download PDF or Print from the full request view.

5. Step-by-step: TARF (travel)

Complete sections from top to bottom. Fields marked required on screen must be filled before the system accepts the submission.

Requester

Name / EmailPre-filled when possible; correct if outdated.
College / Office / ProjectChoose from list, or Other and type the unit.

Details of travel

Type / purpose categoryRequired dropdown.
Event / purposeDescribe the program, training, conference, etc.
Person/s to travelSelect people from the directory (search helps), and/or list Additional travelers (guests, students).
Destination(s)Where travel occurs.
JustificationExplain why travel is needed.
COS or JOAnswer whether any travelers are COS or JO (required radio).
Departure / return datesReturn must not be before departure.
Supporting documentsOptional — up to 10 files (PDF, Word, or images).

Endorsement & publicity

Applicable endorserRequired — pick the role/office that fits your route.
Supervisor / unit emailValid email where endorsement will be routed (e.g. unit mailbox).
Publicity / coverageSelect at least one option (including N/A if listed). Optionally add notes under Other.

Travel type & support

Type of travelRequired radio (e.g. official business vs other types).
Requested supportSelect at least one checkbox or explain under the “Other support” text area.

Funding (appears only for Official Business)

When Type of travel is Official Business, a funding section becomes required:

  • Funding charged to and Funding specifier (choose or specify Other).
  • Endorser for fund availability — Budget or Accounting role as instructed on the form.
  • Total estimated amount (IOT + LIB).
  • Optional uploads: itinerary (IOT), line item budget (LIB).

Click Submit TARF. Fix any error message shown before resubmitting.

7. Tips and common issues

  • Emails must be valid. Invalid requester or supervisor/unit email will block submission.
  • Applicants / endorsers not in the map: If the system reports that an applicable endorser is not linked to a portal account, notify your administrator.
  • Fund availability: If Budget or Accounting designate no active officer for your selection, HR may need to assign the role.
  • DTR / pardons: Some attendance adjustments use separate pardon flows (including TARF/NTARF-related pardons); this guide focuses on submitting the activity request forms only.
  • Admin calendar “Add TARF”: That feature (admin calendar) schedules travel vs on-site NTARF for groups in the institutional calendar — it is not the same screen as faculty TARF Request submissions.

For technical setup (database migrations), administrators may reference the project migration runner for TARF and NTARF. End users normally only need this portal page and endorsement contacts.
